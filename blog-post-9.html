Hourly vs Salary: Which Pays Better? Real Math

By Nishikant Xalxo | Finance Expert | Updated: January 10, 2025 | 9 min read

I switched from hourly to salary in 2022 and initially regretted it—until I calculated the total compensation. Here's how to make the real comparison, including hidden factors most people miss.

The Basic Math

Example: $25/hour vs $52,000/year salary 
Hourly: $25/hour × 40 hours/week × 52 weeks = $52,000/year
Salary: $52,000/year ÷ 52 weeks ÷ 40 hours = $25/hour base

Looks equal, but it's not that simple.

Factor 1: Overtime

Hourly worker: Eligible for 1.5x OT after 40 hours/week

Salary worker: Usually exempt from OT (no extra pay)

Scenario Hourly (45h/week) Salary (45h/week)
Base pay $52,000 $52,000
OT pay (5h/week × 1.5) +$9,750 $0
Total $61,750 $52,000

Verdict: If you regularly work >40 hours, hourly pays more.

Factor 2: Benefits Differences

Salary workers typically get:

  • Paid time off (vacation, sick days) - value $3,000-5,000/year
  • Better health insurance - value $2,000-4,000/year
  • 401k/retirement matching - value $1,500-3,000/year
  • Bonus opportunities - $2,000-10,000/year

Total benefits value: $8,500-22,000/year

Real Hourly vs Salary Comparison (at $52k base): 
Hourly (45h/week): $61,750 base + $0 benefits = $61,750
Salary (45h/week): $52,000 base + $15,000 benefits = $67,000

Salary wins by $5,250/year when benefits included.

Factor 3: Job Security

Salary advantages:

  • Harder to lay off (more employment protections)
  • Notice period before termination
  • Severance packages more common
  • Unemployment benefits often higher

Hourly reality: Can be sent home early, schedule reduced without notice.

Factor 4: Career Growth

Salary positions:

  • Clear promotion paths
  • Performance reviews and raises
  • Management opportunities
  • Professional development budget

Hourly reality: Limited advancement, annual raises only track inflation.

Real Decision Matrix

Choose Hourly if:

  • You work exactly 40 hours/week (no more)
  • You need flexibility in schedule
  • You want OT pay for extra hours

Choose Salary if:

  • You work 45+ hours/week consistently
  • Benefits are important to you
  • You want career advancement
  • Job security is priority
